### 16:30–18:00 · Choose One Optional Extension

| Option | What You’ll See | Best For | Planning Note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Wukang Road | Historic villas, tree-lined streets and cafés | Architecture, city walks and photography | Allow transfer time from Lujiazui; best if departure is later. |
| Xintiandi | Restored shikumen lanes, dining and polished public spaces | First-time visitors and an easy evening stop | Good balance of history, dining and transport convenience. |
| Tianzifang | Narrow lanes, small studios, souvenirs and cafés | Crafts and alleyway atmosphere | Can be crowded; allow about 1 hour plus travel time. |
| North Bund | Quieter waterfront and a different skyline angle | Relaxed photography and a low-stress finish | Best option if you want to stay close to the river and central districts. |

### After 18:00 · Departure

- Allow 60–90 minutes at Shanghai Hongqiao Railway Station before departure.

- Use the exact English name and passport number shown on your travel document.

- Carry the original passport; some travelers may need the staffed inspection lane.

- Book through the official railway channel or a foreigner-friendly platform such as Trip.com.

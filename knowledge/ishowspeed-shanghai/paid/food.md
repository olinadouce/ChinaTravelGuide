## Shanghai Food Guide

Benbang cuisine is known for sweet-savory seasoning, soy-based braising and polished home-style cooking. The following choices are accessible for first-time visitors.

| Category | Dish or Experience | Budget Reference | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Classic Benbang | Red-braised pork belly / Hong Shao Rou | RMB 60–120 per dish | Glossy, tender and sweet-savory; a signature local dish. |
| Classic Benbang | Sweet-and-sour pork ribs / Tang Cu Xiao Pai | RMB 50–100 per dish | An approachable starter with balanced sweet and sour flavors. |
| Classic Benbang | Shanghai-style smoked fish / Xun Yu | RMB 50–120 per dish | A sweet-savory cold appetizer; usually served before the main meal. |
| Seasonal soup | Yanduxian | RMB 80–160 per pot | Salted pork, fresh pork and bamboo shoots; especially popular in spring. |
| Noodles | Scallion oil noodles / Cong You Ban Mian | RMB 15–35 per bowl | Simple, local and ideal for a quick lunch. |
| Breakfast / snack | Shengjianbao | RMB 10–30 per serving | Crisp bottom, soft wrapper and juicy pork filling. |
| Dim sum | Xiaolongbao | RMB 25–80 per basket | Eat carefully: the soup inside can be very hot. |

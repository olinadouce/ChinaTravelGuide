## Practical Tips for International Visitors

Documents and Ticketing

• Carry your original passport with you. Hotel check-in, rail travel, scenic-area ticket windows and real-name reservation checks may require the physical passport.

• If an online ticketing page does not accept passports, try Trip.com, Ctrip international version, hotel front-desk assistance or the scenic-area manual ticket counter.

• For Jade Dragon Snow Mountain, Pudacuo, Meili-area vehicles and rail tickets, confirm 1-3 days in advance during peak seasons.

Payment and Cash

• Use Alipay as the main payment tool and WeChat Pay as backup. Carry Visa/Mastercard and RMB 800-2,000 in cash.

• Mountain areas, small restaurants, drivers, Yubeng guesthouses and local transfers may require cash or direct WeChat/Alipay transfers.

• Pay key transport arrangements in advance where possible and keep screenshots.

Altitude and Health

• Kunming and Dali are good low-to-mid altitude entry points. Shangri-La, Feilai Temple, Meili and Yubeng are higher and require slower pacing.

• Avoid alcohol, heavy exercise and overeating on the first night in Shangri-La and Feilai Temple.

• If severe headache, vomiting, chest tightness or confusion appears, stop ascending and seek medical help.

Luggage and Gear

• Do not drag large suitcases into Yubeng or deep old-town lanes. Prepare a small overnight/hiking bag.

• Essential items: non-slip shoes, warm layers, raincoat, power bank, sun protection, lip balm, motion-sickness medicine, stomach medicine and waterproof phone bag.

• For Meili sunrise and winter travel, gloves, hat and down jacket are highly recommended.

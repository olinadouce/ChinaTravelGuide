14-Day Northwest Yunnan Snow Mountain & Gorge Nature Route Route 3 English Handbook

Kunming - Dali - Lijiang - Tiger Leaping Gorge - Shangri-La - Meili Snow Mountain

This handbook is written directly for international visitors. It is not a general sightseeing introduction, but an actionable 14-day nature route guide. Travelers can use it to choose accommodation areas, plan daily sightseeing order, connect transport segments, select restaurants, estimate budgets and handle weather, altitude, road and ticketing backup plans. All prices are reference prices only. Final costs should be checked again according to exact travel dates on official platforms, Trip.com, Ctrip, Qunar, Dianping, hotel front desks, 12306, local transport companies or scenic-area ticket counters.

| Basic Information | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Route Theme | Northwest Yunnan snow mountains, gorges, alpine lakes, Hengduan Mountain landscapes, Jinsha River valley, Jade Dragon Snow Mountain, Pudacuo National Park, Meili Snow Mountain and optional Yubeng hiking. |
| Recommended Duration | 14 days / 13 nights |
| Route Entry / Exit | Recommended entry from Kunming. Exit from Lijiang, Shangri-La or Kunming depending on flights and rail connections. If international flight options are limited, return to Kunming for the final night. |
| Suitable For | International visitors exploring Yunnan in depth for the first time, nature photographers, light-hiking travelers, couples, families and visitors who want snow mountains and gorges rather than city tourism. |
| Best Seasons | April-June for flowers, clear air and moderate temperatures; September-November for drier weather and snow-mountain views. Winter has clearer skies but colder highland conditions. Summer has greener landscapes but more rain and landslide risk on mountain roads. |
| Difficulty | Moderate to High. Includes high-altitude areas, mountain roads, possible long drives, optional hiking and weather-dependent snow-mountain viewing. Shangri-La and Meili require slower pacing. |
| Exchange Rate Used | USD 1 approximately RMB 7.2. USD amounts are rounded and for budgeting only. |

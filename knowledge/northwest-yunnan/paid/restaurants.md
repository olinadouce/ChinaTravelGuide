5A. Expanded Dining Guide: What to Order Without Searching Again

If you are unsure what to order, use this simple rule: in Kunming, try steam-pot chicken and mushrooms; in Dali, try sour-spicy fish, Xizhou baba and milk fan; in Lijiang, try Naxi cuisine, chicken-bean jelly and cured-rib hotpot; in Shangri-La, try yak hotpot, barley cake and hot soups; in Feilai Temple / Yubeng, prioritize warm, simple and digestible meals such as noodle soup, fried rice, vegetables and eggs. At higher altitude, avoid heavy alcohol, very spicy food and overeating on the first night.

5B. Simple Ordering Guide

| Dish | Where to Try | How to Order / Caution | Best Timing |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Steam-pot chicken | Kunming | Ask for a clear soup base and add vegetables if you want a lighter meal. | Arrival dinner or first full meal. |
| Wild mushrooms | Kunming / Lijiang / Shangri-La | Only eat fully cooked mushrooms; avoid unknown roadside mushroom dishes. | Dinner, not before long driving. |
| Sour-spicy fish | Dali | Good shared dish; ask for less spicy if needed. | Dali dinner. |
| Xizhou baba | Xizhou / Dali | Choose sweet or salty; best as snack rather than full meal. | Morning or afternoon snack. |
| Chicken-bean jelly | Lijiang | Can be cold or stir-fried; cold version may not suit sensitive stomachs. | Light lunch or appetizer. |
| Naxi cured-rib hotpot | Lijiang | Filling and salty; good with vegetables and rice. | After snow-mountain day. |
| Yak hotpot | Shangri-La / Deqin | Eat slowly at altitude; do not drink too much alcohol. | Cold evenings. |
| Barley cake / butter tea | Shangri-La | Good for trying Tibetan-style food; butter tea may be heavy for some visitors. | Lunch or dinner side dish. |
| Hot noodle soup | Feilai / Yubeng | Safest choice if appetite is low or weather is cold. | Before/after hiking. |
| Fried rice / egg dishes | Yubeng | Reliable and easy to digest; pre-order with guesthouse. | Hiking-area dinner. |

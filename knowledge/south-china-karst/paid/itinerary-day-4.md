## Day 4 | Yulong River and Ten-Mile Gallery

| Item | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Today’s Focus | Close-up experience of Yangshuo countryside, rivers and karst peaks. |
| Route | Yangshuo town / Yulong River area |
| Overnight | Yangshuo |
| Estimated Cost | RMB 900-3,500 / USD 125-486 (including hotel, bamboo raft, private car and meals) |

Recommended Route (in Suggested Order)

08:00-09:00 | Breakfast and transfer to Yulong River pier (1 hour). Staying in the Yulong River area makes this easier; town stays require taxi/private car.

09:00-11:00 | Yulong River bamboo raft (40-90 minutes). Choose a classic route. Reference: RMB 180-320 per raft (USD 25-44). Waterproof your phone and passport.

11:00-12:00 | Gongnong Bridge / riverside photos (30-60 minutes). Good for river, peak and countryside reflections.

12:00-13:30 | Lunch (1-1.5 hours). Choose Yulong River farmhouse dishes or return to town.

14:00-16:30 | Ten-Mile Gallery and Moon Hill viewpoint (2-2.5 hours). A private car with photo stops is recommended. Long-distance e-bike riding is not recommended for visitors unfamiliar with Chinese roads.

17:00-18:00 | Hotel rest (1 hour). Avoid heat and overexposure.

18:30-20:00 | Dinner (1.5 hours). Choose beer fish, local dishes or a hotel restaurant.

Transport and Distance Notes

Yangshuo town to Yulong River piers by taxi/private car takes about 20-40 minutes and costs RMB 40-120 (USD 6-17).

Half-day private local transport costs about RMB 300-600 (USD 42-83), useful for families, older visitors and non-cyclists.

Bicycle rental is about RMB 20-50 (USD 3-7), e-bike rental about RMB 50-100 (USD 7-14). International visitors should prioritize safety.

Dining Suggestions for the Day

Lunch: Yulong River farmhouse food (RMB 50-120 / USD 7-17).

Dinner: Beer fish (RMB 80-150 / USD 11-21) or riverside dining (RMB 120-250 / USD 17-35).

Accommodation Suggestions for the Day

Continue staying in Yangshuo. If nature is the priority, choose the Yulong River area. If dinner convenience matters, stay on the West Street edge.

Foreign Visitor Notes

Use only official bamboo-raft piers.

Rafts may stop during heavy rain, high water or safety control.

Carry some cash in countryside areas.

Rainy-Day / Low-Energy Alternative

In rain, cancel bamboo rafting and visit Silver Cave or rest at the hotel. If low on energy, do a short raft route plus Gongnong Bridge only.

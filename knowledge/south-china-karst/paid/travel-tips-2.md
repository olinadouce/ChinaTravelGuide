## Practical Tips for International Visitors

Documents and Ticketing

Carry your original passport with you; do not rely only on photos. Hotel check-in, high-speed rail, scenic-area ticket windows and real-name reservation checks may require the physical passport.

If an online ticketing page does not accept passports, try Trip.com, Ctrip international version, hotel front desk assistance or the scenic-area manual ticket counter.

During peak periods, confirm tickets at least 1-3 days in advance, especially for Huangguoshu, Xiaoqikong and the Li River cruise.

Payment and Cash

Use Alipay as the main payment tool and WeChat Pay as a backup. Carry Visa/Mastercard and RMB 500-1,500 in cash. Mountain areas, villages, small stalls and drivers may rely more on cash.

If mobile payment fails, try switching between Alipay and WeChat first, then use cash or ask your hotel for assistance.

Language and Navigation

Save all hotel, attraction, station and airport names in Chinese. Showing drivers Chinese addresses is more reliable than English addresses.

Prepare useful Chinese sentences such as: “Please take me to this hotel,” “I need to buy a ticket with my passport,” “I need the manual ticket counter,” and “Please help me call a car.”

Luggage and Gear

Longji Rice Terraces are not suitable for dragging large suitcases uphill. Prepare a small two-night overnight bag. Essential items include non-slip shoes, raincoat, waterproof phone bag, power bank, mosquito repellent, motion-sickness medicine and stomach medicine.

Huangguoshu and Xiaoqikong have strong moisture and water spray. A raincoat is more practical than an umbrella.

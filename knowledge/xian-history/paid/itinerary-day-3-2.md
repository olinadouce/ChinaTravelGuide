## Day 3 Jianshui → Confucian Temple → Zhu Family Garden → Tuanshan

Folk Houses → Yuanyang → Bada Terraced Fields for sunset

(Approx. 150 km, driving time: 2.5 hours)

08:00—Depart after breakfast at the hotel.

08:30—Visit 【Jianshui Confucian Temple】— one of the largest Confucian temples in

southern China, second only to Qufu Confucian Temple. Built in the Yuan Dynasty, it

features elegant and solemn structures along the central axis: Pan Pool, Lingxing Gate, and

Dacheng Hall, showcasing profound Confucian culture.

09:30—Arrive at 【Zhu Family Garden】, built by the wealthy Zhu brothers in southern

Yunnan during the late Qing Dynasty. Known as the “Grand View Garden of Southern

Yunnan”, it has 214 rooms and 42 courtyards with exquisite wood and brick carvings,

blending Jiangnan garden style with southern Yunnan residential features.

11:00— Head to 【Tuanshan Folk Houses】 – a 600-year-old Yi ethnic village and a

World Monument Heritage site. More than 20 well-preserved Qing Dynasty dwellings

remain, represented by Zhang Family Garden and Huang’en Mansion, with carved

gateways and the classic “three houses with one screen wall” layout intact.

12:00—Lunch

13:00—Depart for 【Yuanyang】. Along the way, you will see colorful tropical fruit stalls

and banana plantations.

16:00—Check in upon arrival in Yuanyang, then go to 【Bada Terraced Fields】 to wait

for sunset. As the sun sets, the thousand-year-old terraces stretch far below your feet.

May–August: green rice waves sway in the wind

Mid-September to early October: golden ripe rice covers the mountains

November–April: water-filled terraces reflect the sunset glow like mirrors

You will marvel at the amazing work of nature and humanity.

19:00—Dinner

Tips:

Yuanyang mountaintop is about 1,900 meters above sea level with a large

temperature difference between day and night. Please keep warm.

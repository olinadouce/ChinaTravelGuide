Three Cities, Three Thousand Years
——4-day 3-night itinerary in Henan Province
Henan, Cradle of China’s 3,000-Year CivilizationStretching across the heart of the
Central Plains, the gentle Luo River winds through this land, where Chinese
civilization first took root and the Yellow River culture has flourished for three
millennia.
Zhengzhou preserves the mysteries of early Huaxia kingdoms; Luoyang radiates the
grandeur of the prosperous Sui and Tang dynasties; Kaifeng revives the bustling glory
of the Song Dynasty’s Eastern Capital. Spanning just three hundred li, this route links
the three ancient capitals of Zhengzhou, Bianjing (Kaifeng) and Luoyang, carrying
travelers straight into the scroll of Chinese history.
Far from rushed, superficial sightseeing, this itinerary delivers an immersive cultural
journey. Savor authentic Henan cuisine, watch intangible cultural heritage operas, and
witness authentic Shaolin Kung Fu performances. Amid stone grottoes, ancient
Buddhist temples and Song imperial palaces, you will trace the enduring cultural
lineage of China spanning three thousand years.
Tour Highlights
I. Visit Three Ancient Capitals in One Trip, Uncover 3,000 Years of Yellow
River & Huaxia Civilization
This route connects three core ancient capitals of the Central Plains — Zhengzhou,
Luoyang and Kaifeng, covering the entire "300 Li, 3,000 Years" Huaxia Cultural
Corridor. A microcosm of Yellow River civilization and early Chinese civilization, it
lets you trace the full timeline of ancient Chinese history in one single journey:
Zhengzhou: The birthplace of Huaxia civilization, where you explore the origins of
early ancient Chinese kingdoms.
Luoyang: The magnificent imperial capital of the prosperous Sui and Tang dynasties,
showcasing the glorious age when envoys from all nations flocked to the Tang
Empire.

Kaifeng: Immerse yourself in the prosperity of the Northern Song’s Eastern Capital
and experience the bustling Song street scenes depicted in ancient Chinese paintings.
Within one trip, you will witness the complete evolution of China, ranging from early
Huaxia civilizations to the golden age of the Tang Dynasty and the prosperous Song
Dynasty, gaining profound insight into the origins of Chinese civilization and the
roots of Yellow River culture.
II. Two UNESCO World Heritage Sites Included, Witness World-Class Cultural
Treasures at One Go
Longmen Grottoes: The world’s largest royal stone carving treasure trove. Thousands
of Buddha statues were carved over more than 400 years, representing the pinnacle of
Chinese Buddhist stone sculpture. A must-see oriental art gem for overseas visitors, it
offers a close look at the unparalleled sculptural craftsmanship of ancient China.
Shaolin Temple: World-famous birthplace of Zen Buddhism and Chinese Kung Fu.
Watch authentic live Shaolin Kung Fu performances and interact with warrior monks
to experience the globally beloved oriental martial arts and Zen culture firsthand.
III. Diverse Cultural Scenes for Rich Travel Content
This itinerary features eight distinctive thematic experiences: stone grotto carvings,
thousand-year-old Buddhist temples, imperial palace ruins, ancient city walls,
Song-style martial arts towns, traditional opera, Chinese martial arts, and authentic
Central Plains cuisine. Blending natural scenery, historical relics, folk customs and
live interactive shows, this trip delivers unique and unforgettable Chinese travel
memories for history study, cultural exploration and photography enthusiasts alike.

Notes:
I. Documents
 Carry your passport with you throughout the trip. Real-name registration is required for hotel check-in and
scenic spot admission. Make sure to complete the temporary accommodation registration for foreigners at the
hotel upon arrival.
 Keep your passport photo, the contact information of the tour guide and the local consulate. You can quickly
reapply for the lost documents.
II. Tourist Site Etiquette (Core Scenic Spots of the Tour Itinerary)
 In the Longmen Grottoes, Baishazhi Temple, Shaolin Temple and grottoes, it is prohibited to expose
shoulders or wear extremely short clothing; flashlights are not allowed for taking photos of Buddha statues,
and touching or climbing the stone-carved cultural relics is not permitted; do not step on the thresholds of the
temples and do not force yourself to take photos with monks.
 In Kaifeng Prefecture, ancient city wall, and the ruins of the Sui and Tang Dynasty Luoyang City, do not
climb the walls of ancient buildings or stone-carved reliefs, and abide by the regulations of the scenic spot's
guardrails.
 At the entrance of the scenic spot, salespeople promoting incense, fortune-telling and private guides can be
directly refused.
III. Climate and Items
 The temperature difference between day and night in Henan is significant. Always carry a thin coat and an
umbrella; when walking in the grottoes and ancient cities, wear comfortable and slip-resistant shoes.
 The travel time between scenic spots is long. It is recommended to bring motion sickness medication.
IV. Public Civilized Behavior Standards
 Smoking is prohibited indoors and in the entire area of the scenic spot, only designated outdoor areas are
allowed to smoke; loud talking and cutting in line are prohibited in public places.
 When shooting local residents or monks, obtain their consent in advance and do not randomly shoot in
controlled areas.

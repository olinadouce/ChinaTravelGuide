## Tour Highlights

I. Visit Three Ancient Capitals in One Trip, Uncover 3,000 Years of Yellow River & Huaxia Civilization

This route connects three core ancient capitals of the Central Plains — Zhengzhou, Luoyang and Kaifeng, covering the entire "300 Li, 3,000 Years" Huaxia Cultural Corridor. A microcosm of Yellow River civilization and early Chinese civilization, it lets you trace the full timeline of ancient Chinese history in one single journey:

Zhengzhou: The birthplace of Huaxia civilization, where you explore the origins of early ancient Chinese kingdoms.

Luoyang: The magnificent imperial capital of the prosperous Sui and Tang dynasties, showcasing the glorious age when envoys from all nations flocked to the Tang Empire.

Kaifeng: Immerse yourself in the prosperity of the Northern Song’s Eastern Capital and experience the bustling Song street scenes depicted in ancient Chinese paintings.

Within one trip, you will witness the complete evolution of China, ranging from early Huaxia civilizations to the golden age of the Tang Dynasty and the prosperous Song Dynasty, gaining profound insight into the origins of Chinese civilization and the roots of Yellow River culture.

II. Two UNESCO World Heritage Sites Included, Witness World-Class Cultural Treasures at One Go

Longmen Grottoes: The world’s largest royal stone carving treasure trove. Thousands of Buddha statues were carved over more than 400 years, representing the pinnacle of Chinese Buddhist stone sculpture. A must-see oriental art gem for overseas visitors, it offers a close look at the unparalleled sculptural craftsmanship of ancient China.

Shaolin Temple: World-famous birthplace of Zen Buddhism and Chinese Kung Fu. Watch authentic live Shaolin Kung Fu performances and interact with warrior monks to experience the globally beloved oriental martial arts and Zen culture firsthand.

III. Diverse Cultural Scenes for Rich Travel Content

This itinerary features eight distinctive thematic experiences: stone grotto carvings, thousand-year-old Buddhist temples, imperial palace ruins, ancient city walls, Song-style martial arts towns, traditional opera, Chinese martial arts, and authentic Central Plains cuisine. Blending natural scenery, historical relics, folk customs and live interactive shows, this trip delivers unique and unforgettable Chinese travel memories for history study, cultural exploration and photography enthusiasts alike.

| Today's Tour: Longmen Grottoes, Baima Temple, Lijingmen Cross Street Old Town, Sui-Tang Luoyang City (Yingtian Gate, Mingtang Tianfang exterior scenes) Morning: Take a bus to Luoyang, the ancient capital of thirteen dynasties, to visit the World Cultural Heritage site, a national key cultural relic protection unit, a national AAAA-level tourist attraction, the largest royal stone carving art treasure house in the world, and one of the four major grottoes in China - [Longmen Grottoes] (the visiting time is approximately 2.5 hours). The Longmen Grottoes were carved during the reign of Emperor Xiaowen of the Northern Wei Dynasty, and were continuously constructed for over 400 years. It is the grotto with the most statues and the largest scale in the world, and has been rated by UNESCO as "the peak of Chinese stone carving art". As you enter the grottoes, each Buddha statue seems to have life. Their eyes are filled with either compassion, tranquility, or majesty. Each one is telling a different story. Midday: Have lunch at Luoyang. Afternoon: Visit the "First Temple in the World", the "First Ancient Monastery in China", the "World-renowned Pagoda", and the "First Official Monastery in China" - the [Bai Ma Temple] (tour duration approximately 1.5 hours). Originally, the Bai Ma Temple was a royal temple. Emperor Ming of Han had a dream of a holy monk and sent people to search for him. Later, with a white horse carrying scriptures returning, it was established. It was built in the 11th year of the Eastern Han Dynasty (68 AD), being the first official monastery built after Buddhism was introduced to China. It is the "origin" (the birthplace of Buddhism) and "ancestral temple" (the temple of the master) for China, Vietnam, Korea, Japan, and countries in Europe and America. Korea, Singapore, and Malaysia all came here to take monastic vows. The United States, Germany, and Canada all attended the enthronement ceremony of the abbot of this temple. As the most internationalized temple, the Bai Ma Temple truly deserves the title of "The First Temple in the World". Evening: Stroll along the [Lijingmen Cross Old Street](about 2 hours). Lijingmen is the symbol of Luoyang Ancient City. It has existed since the Jin Dynasty and the Song Dynasty and has witnessed the changes of dynasties as well as the rise and fall of prosperity. The entire Lijingmen scenic area consists of the city gate tower, the moat, the arrow tower, the city wall, the Lijing Bridge (which was a suspension bridge in ancient times), the moat river, etc. It is one of the most distinctive symbols of the historical culture of Luoyang Ancient City. If you have time, you can also visit the Ruins of the Sui-Tang Luoyang City Park, the Tianzhou Gate Square, and the exterior scenes of the Mingtang Paradise. | Today's Tour: Shaolin Temple, Tallinn, Shaolin Kung Fu Performance, Wanshi Mountain - The Great Song Dynasty Martial Arts City Morning: After breakfast, we will take a bus to Dengfeng, the hometown of martial arts. We will visit the World Cultural Heritage Site, a National Key Cultural Relics Protection Unit, a National AAAA-level Tourist Attraction, the birthplace of Chinese martial arts, the ancestral temple of Chan Buddhism, and the most famous temple in the world -[Shaolin Temple] (the visiting time is approximately 2.5 hours). Shaolin Temple was founded in the Northern Wei Dynasty (525 AD). The 28th Patriarch of the Western Tathagata Bodhidharma came to China from South India and spent nine years facing the wall in Shaolin Temple, initiating Chan Buddhism. Thus, Shaolin Temple was honored by the world's Buddhist community as the "Ancestral Temple of Chan Buddhism", and Master Bodhidharma was revered as the "First Patriarch of Chan Buddhism in the Eastern Land". In the early Tang Dynasty, the thirteen Shaolin monks who saved Emperor Li Shimin of the Qin Dynasty achieved unprecedented development and earned the reputation of "The Most Famous Temple in the World". "Chinese Kung Fu Dominates the World, Kung Fu from the World Emerges from Shaolin", this is the birthplace of Shaolin Kung Fu.

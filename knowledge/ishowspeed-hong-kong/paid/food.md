## Hong Kong Food Guide

| Category | Dish or Experience | Budget Reference | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Dim Sum / Yum Cha | Har gow, siu mai, char siu bao, rice rolls and turnip cake | HK$100-400 pp | Order 2–3 baskets per person and share. Tea and service charges may be added separately. |
| Wonton Noodles | Shrimp wonton noodles, beef brisket noodles or mixed noodles | HK$55–200 per bowl | A classic quick meal. Traditional portions may be small, so larger appetites can add vegetables or extra toppings. |
| Hong Kong Roast Meats | Roast goose, char siu, crispy pork belly and soy-sauce chicken | HK$75–200 pp for rice; HK$220–1000 for a shared goose portion | Solo travellers can order roast meat over rice, while groups can share a mixed platter or quarter goose. |
| Cha Chaan Teng | Macaroni soup, satay beef noodles, scrambled eggs, toast, sandwiches and milk tea | HK$35–300 pp | Breakfast and afternoon-tea sets are usually the best value. Iced drinks may cost slightly more. |
| Bakery Snacks | Egg tart, pineapple bun, pineapple bun with butter and cocktail bun | HK$25-100 per item | Ideal for a quick snack. Egg tarts are best eaten warm, while pineapple buns normally contain no pineapple. |
| Cart Noodles | Noodles with fish balls, beef brisket, radish, mushrooms, chicken wings or vegetables | HK$55–200 per bowl | Choose the noodle type, soup base and toppings. Check ingredients carefully if you do not eat offal or pork products. |
| Street Snacks and Desserts | Curry fish balls, fish siu mai, egg waffles, tofu pudding and mango pomelo sago | HK$20–60 per item | Good for sharing while exploring markets. Smaller stalls may prefer cash or local electronic payment methods. |
| Claypot Rice | Chinese sausage and pork-rib rice, chicken and mushroom rice or preserved-meat rice | HK$85–180 per pot | Cooked to order and especially popular in cooler weather. Mix in the soy sauce and try the crispy rice crust at the bottom. |
| Dai Pai Dong / Seafood | Cantonese stir-fries, salt-and-pepper squid, steamed fish, prawns or lobster noodles | HK$120–250 pp for stir-fries; HK$250–1000 pp for seafood | Best for groups. For live seafood, confirm the weight, unit price, cooking charge and estimated total before ordering. |

Budget references are approximate and vary by restaurant, season, serving size and number of diners.

## Day 1 · Pandas, Local Culture and Victoria Peak

| Time | Attraction / Experience | Suggested Plan | Practical Note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 08:00–09:00 | Causeway Bay Breakfast & Times Square | Start with a cha chaan teng breakfast near Times Square. Try a pineapple bun, egg sandwich, Hong Kong-style milk tea or Vitasoy, then walk around the Times Square area. | Speed began his Hong Kong stream in Causeway Bay. Morning streets are generally less crowded and easier for orientation. |
| 09:00–09:40 | Villain Hitting under the Canal Road Flyover | Walk to the traditional villain-hitting area beneath the Canal Road Flyover. Observe respectfully or join an experience after confirming the price with the practitioner. | This is a traditional folk practice rather than a staged tourist show. Ask for permission before taking close-up photos or videos. |
| 09:40–10:20 | Transfer to Ocean Park | Take the MTR from Causeway Bay to Admiralty, change to the South Island Line and continue to Ocean Park Station. | Check the park calendar before departure because opening days and operating hours may change. |
| 10:20–14:30 | Ocean Park Panda Experience | Focus on the giant panda facilities, animal exhibits and selected park attractions. Include the cable car only if queues and weather conditions permit. | Animal visibility cannot be guaranteed. The park is large, so avoid trying to visit every attraction during a half-day visit. |
| 14:30–15:00 | Lunch / Light Meal | Have a light meal inside Ocean Park or near Wong Chuk Hang before continuing the route. | Keep the meal relatively light because the afternoon includes outdoor activities and travel to Victoria Peak. |
| 15:00–16:00 | Wong Chuk Hang Recreation Ground | Visit the public recreation ground where Speed played football and basketball. Walk around the sports facilities or join a game only when invited. | Public pitches may be occupied by schools, clubs or local players. Do not interrupt organised games or film people without consent. |
| 16:00–17:00 | Transfer to Victoria Peak | Travel by taxi or bus toward Central, then take the Peak Tram or continue by taxi to the Peak. | Afternoon traffic and Peak Tram queues can be significant. Allow extra transfer time on weekends and public holidays. |
| 17:00–19:00 | Victoria Peak | Visit the Peak Tower area and enjoy panoramic views of Victoria Harbour and the Hong Kong skyline. Stay through sunset when weather and visibility are suitable. | Sky Terrace 428 requires a ticket. Free viewpoints are also available around the Peak Tower and nearby walking areas. |
| 19:00–19:45 | Transfer to North Point | Descend from the Peak and travel to North Point by taxi, bus or MTR. | Peak Tram queues may be longer after sunset; a taxi or bus may be faster during busy periods. |
| 19:45–21:00 | North Point Local Food Experience | Visit a local cha chaan teng or bakery. Try pineapple buns, egg sandwiches, wonton noodles, milk tea, Vitasoy or other Hong Kong comfort food. | Restaurant opening hours vary. Popular local restaurants may require queuing and may accept cash or Octopus more readily than foreign cards. |
| After 21:00 | Return to Hotel | Return to accommodation in Causeway Bay, Wan Chai, North Point or Central. | Rest early before the longer cross-city route on Day 2. |

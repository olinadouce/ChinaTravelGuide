## Day 2 · Trams, Monkeys, Street Food and Victoria Harbour

| Time | Attraction / Experience | Suggested Plan | Practical Note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 08:30–09:30 | Man Mo Temple | Visit the historic temple in Sheung Wan, observe its incense coils and traditional worship spaces, and experience a quieter side of Hong Kong Island. | Dress and behave respectfully. Avoid blocking worshippers and ask before photographing religious activities. |
| 09:30–10:30 | Hong Kong Tram Experience | Board a regular double-decker tram around Western Market or Sheung Wan and ride east through Central, Wan Chai and Causeway Bay. | Pay when exiting. Sit on the upper deck for better street views and keep your belongings secure. |
| 10:30–11:30 | Transfer to Kam Shan Country Park | Leave the tram route and travel toward Kam Shan Country Park by MTR, bus and taxi. | Kam Shan is outside the main tourist districts. Carry water and allow sufficient transfer time. |
| 11:30–13:30 | Kam Shan Country Park | Walk a short reservoir or family route and observe wild macaques from a safe distance. | Do not feed, touch, chase or pose closely with macaques. Keep food hidden and avoid carrying visible plastic bags. |
| 13:30–15:00 | Lunch & Transfer to Yau Ma Tei | Return toward Kowloon and have lunch near Yau Ma Tei or Jordan. Choose wonton noodles, roast meats, dim sum or a cha chaan teng meal. | Wash or sanitise your hands after the country-park visit. Allow extra time for buses and traffic. |
| 15:00–16:30 | Rest / Flexible City Time | Check in, collect luggage or rest near Jordan, Yau Ma Tei or Tsim Sha Tsui before the evening programme. | This buffer prevents the evening route from becoming rushed if the country-park transfer takes longer than expected. |
| 16:30–18:00 | Temple Street Night Market | Browse the market streets and try beef offal, curry fish balls, siu mai, egg waffles, claypot rice or sweet soup. | The market becomes livelier from late afternoon into the evening. Compare prices before buying souvenirs. |
| 18:00–18:30 | Transfer to Tsim Sha Tsui | Take the MTR or taxi to the Tsim Sha Tsui waterfront. | Walking from the station to the waterfront can take 10–20 minutes depending on the exit used. |
| 18:30–19:30 | Avenue of Stars | Walk along the promenade, see the Bruce Lee statue and photograph the Victoria Harbour skyline during sunset or blue hour. | The waterfront can be crowded. Keep pathways clear and allow extra time for photos. |
| 19:30–20:30 | Victoria Harbour Cruise | Join a harbour sightseeing cruise or take the Star Ferry for a shorter and more affordable harbour experience. | Cruise departure times, piers and routes vary. Confirm the boarding pier and arrive at least 20–30 minutes early. |
| After 20:30 | Departure | Travel to West Kowloon Station, Hong Kong International Airport, a land-border checkpoint. | For an early flight or train, skip the cruise or move it to Day 1. Allow additional time for immigration, security and baggage. |

## Recommended Restaurants

| Restaurant | Type | Best For | Suggested Dishes | Budget | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tim Ho Wan — Sham Shui Po | Dim sum | Good-value MICHELIN Bib Gourmand dim sum | Baked BBQ pork buns, shrimp dumplings, siu mai and rice noodle rolls | HK$80–150 pp | The Sham Shui Po branch is the best-known location. Expect queues during lunch and weekends. |
| Mak Man Kee Noodle Shop — Jordan | Wonton noodles | Traditional Cantonese noodles and a quick local meal | Shrimp wonton noodles, shrimp-roe noodles and beef brisket noodles | HK$50–90 pp | The bowls are traditionally quite small, so travellers with larger appetites may want an additional dish. |
| Kam’s Roast Goose — Wan Chai | Cantonese roast meats | A signature roast-goose meal and MICHELIN-starred dining at a moderate price | Roast goose, char siu and crispy roast pork | HK$150–300 pp | Seating is limited and queues are common. A roast-meat rice plate suits solo travellers, while groups can share a half goose. |
| Hing Kee Restaurant — Temple Street | Claypot rice and Cantonese dishes | A casual evening meal near the Temple Street Night Market | Chinese sausage claypot rice, chicken claypot rice and fried oyster pancake | HK$80–180 pp | Claypot rice is cooked to order and may require a wait. The restaurant offers more than 60 topping combinations. |
| Australia Dairy Company — Jordan | Cha chaan teng | A classic Hong Kong breakfast or quick lunch | Scrambled eggs with toast, ham macaroni soup, egg sandwich and steamed milk pudding | HK$45–80 pp | Service is extremely fast, table sharing is common and diners are generally expected to order promptly. |

## Public Transport and Ticketing Instructions for International Visitors

- High-speed rail tickets: the most reliable options are 12306 English version, Trip.com or a staffed station ticket window. Use Passport as the ID type. Carry your original passport on the travel day; station entry, security and ticket checks may all require it.

- Mobile payment: Alipay is usually the most practical primary payment tool, with WeChat Pay as backup. Scenic areas, cableways, restaurants and taxis usually support QR payment, but small mountain shops and temporary drivers may still prefer cash.

- On-site ticketing: if an online real-name system does not accept a foreign passport, ask the hotel front desk for help or use the staffed scenic-area ticket window. During Chinese holidays, do not rely only on same-day walk-in tickets.

- Internal scenic transport: Huangshan requires scenic shuttle buses before cableways; Huangshan cableways, Sanqingshan cableways and Wuyishan shuttles / bamboo rafts all depend on same-day opening, weather and queue conditions.

- Chinese information preparation: save hotel names, scenic-area names, gate names, cableway names and railway-station names in Chinese. Showing a driver Chinese addresses is more reliable than English addresses.

- Luggage: for Huangshan mountain-top stays, take only a small backpack. Store large suitcases at Tangkou, Huangshan North, a hotel or with a driver. Also keep Sanqingshan and Wuyishan scenic days light.

14-Day East China Mountains, Granite Peaks & Danxia River Route

Huangshan - Sanqingshan - Wuyishan

This handbook is written directly for international visitors. It is designed as an actionable route guide rather than a general sightseeing introduction. You should be able to use it to choose where to stay, understand what each natural site is best known for, follow the daily route order, choose restaurants, connect public transport or private transfers, estimate budgets and use backup plans when weather, crowds or energy levels change. All prices are reference prices only. Before departure, re-check final costs and opening rules on official platforms, Trip.com, Ctrip, 12306, Dianping, hotel front desks or scenic-area ticket counters.

| Basic Information | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Route Theme | Three East China landscape systems: Huangshan granite peaks and sea of clouds, Sanqingshan granite columns and high-altitude cliff walkways, and Wuyishan Danxia cliffs with the Nine-Bend Stream. |
| Recommended Duration | 14 days / 13 nights |
| Route Entry / Exit | Recommended entry from Hangzhou or Shanghai; start the mountain section from Huangshan, continue through Shangrao / Sanqingshan, and exit from Wuyishan, Nanping, Fuzhou or Xiamen. |
| Suitable For | International visitors seeking natural scenery, mountain views, cloud seas, cliff paths, photography, light-to-moderate hiking and scenic river experiences. |
| Best Seasons | April-June and September-November. Spring and early summer are good for mist and greenery; autumn is best for clear skies and colors. Summer is humid and crowded; winter can be quiet but some mountain routes may be affected by ice. |
| Difficulty | Moderate to moderately challenging. There is no major high-altitude sickness risk, but the route includes long stairs, cable cars, mountain shuttles, early starts and weather-sensitive viewpoints. |
| Exchange Rate Used | USD 1 approximately RMB 7.2. USD amounts are rounded and used for budgeting only. |

## Dining Guide: Specific Restaurants, Dishes and Reasons to Choose

| Place | Restaurant / Food Type | Platform Rating Reference | Specialty | Why Recommended | Average Cost |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Huangshan / Tunxi | Laojie Diyi Lou (老街第一楼) | Approx. 4.2-4.6 | Stinky mandarin fish, tofu dishes, Hui-style braised pork | Classic Huangshan Old Street restaurant; good for a first formal Hui cuisine meal. | RMB 80-160 / USD 11-22 |
| Huangshan / Tunxi | Piyun Huifu Cuisine (披云徽府菜) | Approx. 4.2-4.7 | Hui-style banquet dishes, preserved pork, bamboo shoots | Suitable for visitors who want a more complete regional dinner in a stable setting. | RMB 100-220 / USD 14-31 |
| Huangshan / Tunxi | Meishi Renjia / local Hui restaurants | Approx. 4.0-4.5 | Home-style Anhui dishes | Good backup if Old Street restaurants are full. | RMB 60-130 / USD 8-18 |
| Tangkou | Tangkou Hui-style local restaurants near Transfer Center | Approx. 4.0-4.5 | Mountain vegetables, bamboo shoots, chicken soup | Practical before entering Huangshan; choose a busy restaurant near the hotel or transfer center. | RMB 50-120 / USD 7-17 |
| Huangshan Mountain | Mountain hotel buffet / set dinner | Platform ratings vary | Buffet, simple hot dishes, noodles | The most practical option on the mountain. Do not expect city-level dining. | RMB 80-180 / USD 11-25 |
| Huangshan snacks | Huangshan sesame pastry (黄山烧饼) | Approx. 4.0-4.6 | Crispy baked pastry with savory filling | Useful as a light snack for rail transfers or mountain preparation. | RMB 5-20 / USD 1-3 |
| Shangrao / Yushan | Yanshan hot rice noodles (铅山烫粉) shops | Approx. 4.0-4.6 | Rice noodles, spicy broth, toppings | Good breakfast or quick lunch before Sanqingshan transfer. | RMB 15-35 / USD 2-5 |
| Shangrao | Shangrao chicken-leg shops (上饶鸡腿) | Approx. 4.0-4.6 | Braised or roasted chicken legs | Famous local snack, convenient for travel days. | RMB 10-30 / USD 1-4 |
| Sanqingshan foot area | Sanqingshan local restaurants near cableway | Approx. 4.0-4.5 | Free-range chicken soup, bamboo shoots, local greens | Practical after a mountain day; choose restaurants with visible menus and crowds. | RMB 60-130 / USD 8-18 |
| Wuyishan | Yan Wuyi / Wuyi local cuisine restaurants | Approx. 4.2-4.7 | Smoked goose, red-eye fish, tea-flavored dishes | Good first dinner in Wuyishan; lets visitors taste the tea-mountain cuisine style. | RMB 80-180 / USD 11-25 |
| Wuyishan | Langu smoked goose shops (岚谷熏鹅) | Approx. 4.0-4.6 | Wuyishan smoked goose | Signature local flavor; spicy and smoky, best shared by a group. | RMB 60-150 / USD 8-21 |
| Wuyishan | Wuyi Mountain Villa / resort restaurants | Approx. 4.2+ | Refined Fujian / tea cuisine | Reliable for higher-budget visitors or those who prefer calmer dining. | RMB 150+ / USD 21+ |
| Wuyishan | Zixi rice-noodle shops (紫溪粉) | Approx. 4.0-4.5 | Local rice noodles | Simple breakfast or lunch before scenic entry. | RMB 15-40 / USD 2-6 |
| Wuyishan tea experience | Tea houses near Sangu / Dahongpao area | Approx. 4.0-4.7 | Wuyi rock tea tasting | Best paired with the Dahongpao walking day; confirm prices before sitting down. | RMB 50-300+ / USD 7-42+ |

| Ordering guide: In Huangshan, prioritize stinky mandarin fish, Mao tofu, preserved pork with bamboo shoots, mountain vegetables and Huangshan pastry. In the Sanqingshan / Shangrao section, try Yanshan hot rice noodles, Shangrao chicken legs, local chicken soup and bamboo-shoot dishes. In Wuyishan, prioritize Langu smoked goose, red-eye fish, Zixi rice noodles, Wuyi rock tea and tea-flavored dishes. Restaurant ratings are reference ranges from public platforms such as Dianping, Ctrip and Trip.com; search the Chinese name again before departure to confirm branch status, opening hours and queues. |

## Scenic Highlights, Activities and Backup Plan Cards

This section is the quick index for the whole nature route. If your time is limited, prioritize the “Do Not Miss” items. If weather, fog, crowds or fatigue affect the day, use the backup column directly.

| Scenic Site | Why It Is Attractive | What to Do Inside | Recommended Time | Backup Plan |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Shixin Peak / Beihai, Huangshan | Ancient Huangshan pines, compact granite peaks and classic cloud-sea viewpoints. | Walk the Shixin Peak loop, photograph black pine silhouettes, look for Dream Brush Flower and Lion Peak area viewpoints. | 2-3 hrs | If crowded or foggy, shorten to Beihai - Shilin Hotel - Paiyun Pavilion. |
| Xihai Grand Canyon, Huangshan | The most dramatic deep-valley section on Huangshan, with steep cliffs, layered peaks and narrow paths. | Enter from Paiyun Pavilion, walk the upper canyon, use the monorail if descending deeply, avoid rushing. | 3-5 hrs | Skip in thunderstorms, icy conditions or low energy; use Bright Summit / Beihai short route. |
| Bright Summit / Aoyu Peak, Huangshan | One of the best high viewpoints to connect sunrise, sunset, cloud seas and mountain layers. | Use as the central transition point between Beihai, Baiyun and Yuping. Good for sunset if staying nearby. | 1-2 hrs | If wind or fog is heavy, move slowly to the hotel or descend early. |
| Yuping / Greeting Pine, Huangshan | The symbol of Huangshan, with close-up pine-and-rock scenery. | See Greeting Pine, walk Hundred-Step Cloud Ladder if open and energy allows, descend by Yuping Cableway. | 1.5-2.5 hrs | If queues are severe, descend via an alternative cableway or reverse the route. |
| Nanqing Garden, Sanqingshan | The essential Sanqingshan section with Goddess Peak and Python Emerging from the Mountain. | Follow the loop after cableway arrival; stop at major platforms instead of rushing every minor viewpoint. | 2.5-4 hrs | In fog, still walk the short loop because rocks may appear and disappear dramatically. |
| Sunshine Coast / West Coast, Sanqingshan | Cliff walkways with open granite peak views and a lighter walking rhythm than Huangshan. | Choose one or both coast routes depending on energy. Excellent for photos when clouds move fast. | 2-4 hrs | If afraid of heights, reduce West Coast; if cableway time is tight, do Sunshine Coast only. |
| Tianyou Peak, Wuyishan | Best high viewpoint over the Nine-Bend Stream and red Danxia river valley. | Climb early, rest at platforms, look down at river bends and bamboo rafts. | 2.5-4 hrs | If rain or low energy, replace with Wuyi Palace, Jade Maiden Peak viewpoint or a tea-route walk. |
| Nine-Bend Stream Bamboo Raft, Wuyishan | The signature low-altitude river experience: see red cliffs from the water. | Reserve a time slot, arrive early, keep valuables dry and sit balanced on the raft. | 1.5-2 hrs | If rafting stops, walk Wuyi Palace + Dahongpao or climb Tianyou if weather allows. |
| Dahongpao - Water Curtain Cave Trail, Wuyishan | Tea valley, rock walls and shaded walking; the best “tea landscape” section. | Walk slowly through tea fields, observe cliff tea-growing areas and finish near Water Curtain Cave. | 2.5-4 hrs | If rain is heavy, do a shorter tea tasting and skip slippery side paths. |
| One-Line-Sky / Tiger Roaring Rock, Wuyishan | Narrow rock passages, caves, Danxia stairs and compact adventure feel. | Best for active visitors who want a more physical Danxia walk after the easier raft day. | 2-4 hrs | Avoid if claustrophobic, knee-sensitive or after heavy rain; use Wuyi Palace and tea route instead. |

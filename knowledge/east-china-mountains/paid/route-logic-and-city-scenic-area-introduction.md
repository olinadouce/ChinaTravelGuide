## Route Logic and City / Scenic Area Introduction

This route connects three of East China’s most distinctive natural landscapes. Huangshan is about cloud seas, ancient pines, oddly shaped granite rocks and mountain-summit viewpoints. Sanqingshan is about lighter, sharper granite peak clusters and suspended cliff walkways. Wuyishan is a lower-altitude Danxia river landscape where the best experience is to see water from the mountain and see red cliffs from a bamboo raft. Compared with western China routes, this trip has lower altitude risk, but it still requires careful planning because mountain shuttles, cable cars, rail transfers and bamboo-raft reservations all matter.

Natural Scenery Logic: Why This Route Works as a Pure Nature Route

This is not simply a route that links three famous mountains. It deliberately shows three different landscape structures: Huangshan is a high granite mountain with cloud seas and pine-covered cliffs; Sanqingshan is a refined granite peak-and-cliff-walk system with easier high-altitude loops; Wuyishan is a red sandstone Danxia valley where the river route is as important as the mountain route. For this reason, the daily plan explains not only where to go, but also what you should look for, where to stand, how deeply to explore and what to do if visibility or energy is poor.

| Huangshan granite cloud-sea landscape | Shixin Peak, Beihai, Xihai Grand Canyon, Bright Summit, Yuping | High granite peaks, pine trees growing from rock cracks, strange rocks and frequent cloud or mist effects. Huangshan is the most dramatic mountain-view section of the route. | Take the Yungu Cableway uphill; walk Shixin Peak for pine and rock views; enter the upper Xihai Grand Canyon from Paiyun Pavilion; watch sunset at Bright Summit or Paiyun Pavilion; see the Greeting Pine at Yuping. | Avoid the Xihai deep canyon during thunderstorms. If visibility is extremely low, use Emerald Valley, hot spring area or a short Beihai-Yuping route instead. |
| Sanqingshan high cliff-walk granite peaks | Nanqing Garden, Sunshine Coast, West Coast, Sanqing Temple area | The peak clusters are compact and elegant. Cliff walkways allow visitors to see Goddess Peak, Python Emerging from the Mountain, cloud seas and forests without constant steep climbing. | Take Jinsha or Waishuangxi Cableway; visit Nanqing Garden for the two signature rocks; walk Sunshine Coast for open peak views; use West Coast for dramatic cliff paths; add Sanqing Temple or Wanshou Garden if energy is good. | If afraid of heights, reduce time on the West Coast. In heavy fog, focus on Nanqing Garden short route. If cableway queues are long, reduce the day to one main loop. |
| Wuyishan Danxia river valley | Tianyou Peak, Nine-Bend Stream, Dahongpao, Water Curtain Cave, One-Line-Sky, Tiger Roaring Rock | Wuyishan has lower altitude but highly distinctive red sandstone cliffs. The Nine-Bend Stream winds between rock walls, tea valleys and forested slopes. | Climb Tianyou Peak for the best river overview; take the Nine-Bend Stream bamboo raft; walk the Dahongpao - Rock-Bone Flower-Fragrance trail; see Water Curtain Cave; add One-Line-Sky and Tiger Roaring Rock for cave and Danxia steps. | If bamboo rafting stops, prioritize Tianyou Peak and Dahongpao. In heavy rain, reduce Tiger Roaring Rock. For low-energy visitors, choose Wuyi Palace, Jade Maiden Peak viewpoint and a tea experience. |

| Wuyishan North / Nanping / Fuzhou / Xiamen | Final transport buffer options.

## Public Transport and Ticketing for International Visitors

- High-speed rail / trains: use the 12306 English website, 12306 App, Trip.com or a station manual counter. The ticket document must match the travel document; international visitors usually use passports for ticketing and station entry.

- Station entry: some gates can read passports, but if the gate fails, use the manual lane and show your passport plus booking order.

- Ride-hailing: within cities, use Alipay/WeChat ride-hailing or DiDi international version. In remote areas, ask the hotel or driver to help call a car.

- Hired cars: From Dunhuang to Dachaidan, Chaka, Qinghai Lake and Xining, book a licensed car/driver in advance. Confirm route, daily start/end points, tolls, parking fees and driver accommodation costs.

- On-site scenic tickets: If a scenic-area online page does not accept passports, try a manual ticket window, Trip.com/Ctrip international version, or hotel assistance. During peak season, do not rely only on same-day tickets.

- Payment: Use Alipay as the main tool and WeChat Pay as backup. Carry RMB 500-1,500 cash because remote towns, lake areas, small restaurants and drivers may not always handle foreign-card mobile payment smoothly.

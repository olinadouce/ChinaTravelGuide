14-Day Gansu & Qinghai Danxia, Desert and Plateau Lakes Nature Route

Lanzhou - Zhangye - Jiayuguan - Dunhuang - Dachaidan - Chaka - Qinghai Lake - Xining

This handbook is written directly for international visitors planning a nature-focused journey across northwest China. It is not a general sightseeing article. It is an actionable 14-day route guide covering foreigner-friendly accommodation choices, intercity and scenic-area transport, daily sightseeing order, dining recommendations, estimated budgets, backup plans and practical reminders. All prices are reference prices only. Final costs should be checked again according to exact travel dates on official platforms, Trip.com, Ctrip, 12306, hotel front desks, scenic-area ticket counters or licensed drivers.

| Price note: Hotel prices are estimated for standard double rooms, standard twin rooms or basic room categories, not suites, family rooms or executive rooms. Price differences are shown by season where possible. USD 1 is estimated at RMB 7.2 for budgeting only. |

| Basic Information | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Route Theme | Northwest China nature landscapes: ink-style Danxia, rainbow Danxia, Gobi desert, sand dunes, wind-eroded Yardang landforms, salt lakes, plateau lakes and red-rock lake valleys. |
| Recommended Duration | 14 days / 13 nights |
| Entry / Exit | Enter from Lanzhou and leave from Xining. The route can also be done in reverse if flight schedules are better. |
| Suitable For | International visitors who want to focus on China’s northwest scenery, photographers, geology lovers, couples, small groups and families who can accept long driving distances. |
| Best Seasons | May-June and September-October are generally the most comfortable. July-August has stronger tourist demand and higher prices. Winter is quieter but colder, with more weather risk. |
| Difficulty | Moderate to moderately demanding. No extreme hiking is required, but the route includes long road transfers, dry climate, strong sun, wind, desert walking and some high-altitude lake areas. |
| Transport Logic | High-speed rail for major city-to-city segments; taxis or hired cars for scenic areas; hired car is recommended from Dunhuang into the Qinghai lake section. |
| Exchange Rate Used | USD 1 approximately RMB 7.2. USD amounts are rounded estimates. |

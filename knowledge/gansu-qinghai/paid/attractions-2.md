## Scenic Highlights, Must-See Points and Internal Activities

| Attraction | Most Distinctive Feature | Must-See Point | What You Can Do Inside | Suggested Time | Backup Plan |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Lanzhou Ink Danxia | Soft red sandstone hills, ravines and distant layers. | Jiuse Danqing, Silk Road sunset-style viewpoints and wide ridgeline views. | Take scenic shuttle between platforms; photograph layers, valleys and distant hills. Deep hiking is not necessary. | 3-5 hrs | If windy or hazy, switch to Yellow River waterfront, Baita Mountain viewpoint or hotel rest. |
| Zhangye Colorful Danxia | Multicolored sedimentary layers like a geological layer cake. | Sunset platforms, color-banded valleys and Qilian Mountain background. | Use the shuttle route; focus on panoramic platforms; do not try to walk between distant platforms. | 3-4 hrs | If cloudy, still visit for structure; if sandstorm, shorten the stay. |
| Binggou Danxia | Castle, palace and column-shaped red rock formations. | Louvre-like formations, camel-shaped rocks and palace outlines. | Shuttle + short walks; best paired with Colorful Danxia to compare color versus shape. | 2.5-4 hrs | Fit visitors may choose Pingshan Lake instead; do not combine both if tired. |
| Pingshan Lake Grand Canyon | Deep canyon, red walls and close-up rock corridors. | Canyon floor perspective, red cliffs and narrow passages. | Walk down/up stairs, enter the valley floor, photograph rock walls close-up; protect knees. | 4-5 hrs | Older visitors, families or low-energy travelers should choose Binggou. |
| Mingsha Mountain and Crescent Spring | Sand dunes and a crescent-shaped oasis spring in one desert scene. | Sunset from the dune ridge and overlooking Crescent Spring from above. | Camel ride, sand-hill climbing, shoe-cover rental, sunset photography. | 3-5 hrs | If wind is strong, keep to lower areas and skip climbing high dunes. |
| Mogao Caves | Protected Silk Road cave art with statues and murals. | Officially opened caves, digital exhibition and guide-led cave visit. | Watch the digital introduction, follow the guided cave route, observe murals and sculptures without touching. | 3-4 hrs | If normal tickets sell out, try emergency tickets or switch to Dunhuang Museum/rest. |
| Dunhuang Yardang | Wind-eroded desert formations shaped by long-term erosion. | Fleet-like, castle-like and animal-like Yardang silhouettes at sunset. | Take scenic shuttle/driver route, photograph silhouettes and Gobi horizons. | 6-8 hrs incl. transport | If weather is poor, shorten the route and return before dark. |
| Dachaidan Emerald Lake | Blue, green and dark mineral salt ponds in a basin landscape. | Windless reflections, color blocks and Qaidam desert edges. | Walk designated paths, photograph reflections, compare different pond colors. | 2-4 hrs | If windy/cloudy, focus on color contrast rather than reflection. |
| Chaka Salt Lake | Mirror-like salt lake reflections on clear windless days. | White salt surface, sky reflection and small tourist train if operating. | Walk on safe designated areas, take reflection photos, use shoe covers if required. | 2-4 hrs | If weather is poor, shorten stay and continue to Qinghai Lake area. |
| Qinghai Lake | Vast lake surface, plateau grassland, road scenery and distant mountains. | Erlangjian/official viewing area, lakeside road views, seasonal rapeseed fields. | Lake photography, short lakeside walk, grassland view stops; avoid unsafe private lake access. | 3-5 hrs | If weather is bad, make it a scenic drive and sleep in Xining. |
| Kanbula | Red-rock cliffs, reservoir/lake valley and forested Danxia landscape. | Red cliffs above water, valley viewpoints and road scenery. | Drive between viewpoints, short walks and lake-valley photography. | 4-6 hrs + transport | Skip if Day 14 departure is early or if weather/traffic is poor. |

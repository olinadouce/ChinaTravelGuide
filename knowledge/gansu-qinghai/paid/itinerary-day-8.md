## Day 8 | Dunhuang Yardang and Gobi Wind-Erosion Landscape

| Item | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Today’s Focus | See Gobi wind-eroded landforms. Yardang’s appeal is how wind cuts earth and rock into fleet-like, castle-like and animal-like shapes. |
| Route | Dunhuang city -> Yumen Pass direction -> Yardang landforms -> Dunhuang city |
| Overnight | Dunhuang |
| Estimated Cost | RMB 800-2,500 / USD 111-347, depending mainly on group tour or hired car. |

Recommended Route (in Suggested Order)

- 08:00-09:00 | Breakfast and supplies. Bring water, snacks, sunglasses, sunblock, wind jacket and power bank.

- 09:00-12:00 | Drive toward Yumen Pass / Gobi corridor. Stop only at safe designated locations.

- 12:00-13:00 | Simple lunch or packed meal.

- 13:00-17:30 | Yardang landform visit. Use the scenic shuttle or driver-arranged route. Focus on shapes and long horizon views.

- 17:30-20:30 | Return to Dunhuang. Avoid late-night return if tired.

Transport and Distance Notes

- Dunhuang to Yardang direction is around 160-200 km one way and takes 2.5-3.5 hours.

- Public transport is not suitable for most international visitors. Full-day hired car or regulated tour: about RMB 600-1,200 / USD 83-167.

- Mobile signal may be weaker. Confirm pickup, route and return time in advance.

Scenic Highlights and What to Do

- Yardang is most impressive when you understand scale: it is not a single rock but an open field of wind-eroded forms across the desert.

- Best visual themes: silhouette, empty road, Gobi horizon, castle shapes and fleet-like rows.

- Do not leave designated areas. The terrain can be fragile and disorienting.

Dining and Supply Suggestions

- Lunch: packed food or simple roadside/scenic meal.

- Dinner: warm meal in Dunhuang after return. Good choices include lamb, hot dishes or noodles.

- Bring more water than usual because this is a dry full-day outing.

Accommodation Suggestions

- Stay in Dunhuang again. Do not drive onward to Dachaidan after a full Yardang day.

- Confirm Day 9 hired car and Dachaidan hotel tonight.

Foreign Visitor Notes / Practical Reminders

- Carry passport and enough cash as backup.

- Do not rely on same-day ride-hailing for remote Gobi attractions.

- Protect eyes and camera from wind and sand.

Weather / Low-Energy / Delay Backup

- Poor weather: shorten the route and return earlier.

- Low energy: use a shorter West Line route or rest in Dunhuang.

- Driver issue: cancel remote Yardang rather than attempting self-arranged transport without confirmation.

## Day 5 | Zhangye to Jiayuguan

| Item | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Today’s Focus | Transition from Danxia scenery to the Gobi-edge Hexi Corridor section. Jiayuguan works as a rest and supply stop between Zhangye and Dunhuang. |
| Route | Zhangye hotel -> Zhangye West/Station -> Jiayuguan South/Station -> Jiayuguan city |
| Overnight | Jiayuguan |
| Estimated Cost | RMB 650-1,800 / USD 90-250, including hotel, rail or hired car, meals and city transport. |

Recommended Route (in Suggested Order)

- 08:30-09:30 | Breakfast and checkout. Pack snacks and water for the coming desert sections.

- 10:00-13:00 | Rail transfer to Jiayuguan, or use a private car if you prefer door-to-door transfer.

- 13:00-15:00 | Check in and lunch/rest in Jiayuguan.

- 15:30-17:30 | Optional light city/Gobi-edge viewing. Keep it low intensity; the route’s focus remains nature, not heavy cultural sightseeing.

- 18:30-20:00 | Dinner and confirm Day 6 transfer to Dunhuang.

Transport and Distance Notes

- Zhangye to Jiayuguan by rail usually takes about 1.5-2.5 hours; fares vary roughly RMB 70-180 / USD 10-25.

- Private car can cost about RMB 700-1,100 / USD 97-153 and takes longer but avoids station transfers.

- Jiayuguan station to city hotel generally takes 15-30 minutes by taxi.

Scenic Highlights and What to Do

- This is a landscape transition day. Along the road/rail corridor, notice the change from Danxia hills to flatter Gobi-edge terrain.

- Optional: view open Gobi light and city-edge desert feeling rather than adding too many historical sites.

Dining and Supply Suggestions

- Lunch: hotel restaurant or local noodle restaurant.

- Dinner: Jiayuguan markets or local lamb/noodle restaurants. Keep dinner warm and simple before the next transfer.

- Buy water, fruit and snacks for Day 6 desert arrival.

Accommodation Suggestions

- Stay in a city hotel rather than a small guesthouse. Confirm passport check-in.

- Pick a hotel that makes railway station access and next-day departure easy.

Foreign Visitor Notes / Practical Reminders

- Do not overload this day. The main purpose is to reduce fatigue before Dunhuang.

- If you are interested in culture, you may briefly visit Jiayuguan Pass, but this route keeps it optional because the project is nature-focused.

Weather / Low-Energy / Delay Backup

- Train delay: reduce all afternoon activity and just rest.

- Low energy: no optional sightseeing; hotel rest only.

- Bad weather: use city meal/supply time and prepare for Dunhuang.

## Route Logic and Nature Landscape Theme

This route is designed around three major northwest-China landscapes: red Danxia and colorful sedimentary hills, desert and wind-eroded landforms, and salt lakes plus highland lakes. The first half in Gansu focuses on Lanzhou Ink Danxia, Zhangye Colorful Danxia, Mingsha Mountain and Crescent Spring, the Mogao Caves environment and Dunhuang Yardang. The second half enters Qinghai and focuses on Dachaidan Emerald Lake, Chaka Salt Lake, Qinghai Lake and optional Kanbula.

The biggest difference between this route and southern China nature routes is scale. Distances are long, towns are far apart, and the road scenery often includes Gobi desert, Qilian Mountain foothills, basins, salt flats and plateau lake areas. You should treat this as a long-distance nature road journey rather than a compact city trip. Book foreigner-friendly hotels in advance, use licensed drivers or reputable booking platforms for remote sections, and avoid night driving on long desert roads.

| Place | Unique Natural Appeal | Travel Advice |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lanzhou Ink Danxia | Soft red sandstone hills, valleys and layered ridges. It is less brightly colored than Zhangye and works well as a gentle Danxia introduction. | Half day is enough. Afternoon light is better for layers and long views. If wind or visibility is poor, switch to the Yellow River waterfront. |
| Zhangye Colorful Danxia | Red, yellow, white, grey and green mineral bands spread across rolling hills like an exposed geological cross-section. | Schedule the core visit 1.5-2 hours before sunset and use the scenic shuttle to move between viewing platforms. |
| Binggou Danxia / Pingshan Lake Grand Canyon | Binggou is more about castle-like rock shapes; Pingshan Lake is more about walking inside a canyon with stairs and close rock walls. | Ordinary visitors should choose Binggou. Strong walkers who want a canyon experience can choose Pingshan Lake. |
| Mingsha Mountain and Crescent Spring | High sand dunes, desert light, camel routes and a crescent-shaped oasis spring appear in the same scene. | Enter in late afternoon to avoid heat. Camel riding, sand-hill climbing and sunset viewing are the main activities. |
| Mogao Caves | A protected World Heritage cave-art site set in the Dunhuang oasis environment. The visit requires strict reservation and conservation rules. | Book early. Follow the official visiting sequence and never touch walls, paintings or sculptures. |
| Dunhuang Yardang | Wind-eroded desert formations shaped like fleets, castles and animals across an open Gobi landscape. | Best done with a driver or regulated day tour. It is far from town; do not self-arrange casually without transport confirmation. |
| Dachaidan Emerald Lake | Salt ponds with blue, green and dark mineral colors. On clear, windless days, the lake looks like a patchwork of gemstones. | Best in good sunlight and low wind. Bring sunglasses and avoid stepping onto unsafe salt crust. |
| Chaka Salt Lake | A salt lake famous for mirror-like reflections when the sky is clear, wind is low and shallow water conditions are right. | Do not expect mirror reflections on windy or cloudy days. White salt surfaces can be very bright; protect eyes and skin. |
| Qinghai Lake | China’s largest lake, with a sea-like water surface, plateau grassland, distant mountains and seasonal rapeseed flowers. | Do not rush it as a photo stop only. The value is the scale of lake, sky, grassland and road scenery. |

## Scenic Highlights: What to See, How to Play and Backup Choices

This section explains what each scenic area is actually worth seeing. A visitor should not need to search separately for what to do inside each attraction.

| Scenic Area | Main Appeal | Recommended Way to Visit | Backup if Not Suitable |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Yuanjiajie | The most iconic high-view stone-pillar forest in Zhangjiajie. Focus on Mihuntai, Qiankun Pillar / Hallelujah Mountain, First Bridge under Heaven and Back Garden. This is usually the first truly dramatic view of the Zhangjiajie landscape. | Enter from Wulingyuan Landmark Gate, take the shuttle to Shuiraosimen, use Bailong Elevator up to Yuanjiajie, then walk Mihuntai - Qiankun Pillar - First Bridge under Heaven - Back Garden. Allow 3-4 hours. Do not queue at every platform; choose the open-view platforms. | If the Bailong Elevator queue is over one hour, go up by Tianzi Mountain cableway first, or visit Golden Whip Stream / Ten-Mile Gallery in the morning and return later. |
| Bailong Elevator | A vertical glass elevator rising along the rock face. It is both transport and a signature Zhangjiajie experience. | Use it one way as an efficient uphill tool. Stand near the glass side if possible, but do not waste too much time in peak queues. | If afraid of heights or queues are too long, use Tianzi Mountain cableway instead. |
| Tianzi Mountain | Wide panoramic views of peak walls, cloud seas and distant sandstone pillars. Key stops: Helong Park, Imperial Brush Peaks, Fairy Scattering Flowers and West Sea Peak Forest. | Visit when visibility improves. Take scenic shuttle to Helong Park, then walk between viewpoints. Allow 2.5-3.5 hours. | If fog is heavy, go to Ten-Mile Gallery or Golden Whip Stream first, then return to higher viewpoints later. |
| Golden Whip Stream | A valley-floor walk looking upward at peaks. It combines forest, stream, monkeys and canyon atmosphere, making it the most natural low-intensity walk in Zhangjiajie. | Walk the classic Forest Park Gate - Golden Whip Rock - Zicao Pool - Shuiraosimen section in 2.5-4 hours. Low-energy visitors can walk 1-2 hours and turn back. | In heavy rain or rising water, reduce valley walking and switch to Huanglong Cave, Baofeng Lake or hotel rest. |
| Yangjiajie | More rugged, wilder and less crowded. Best for travelers who like narrow paths, peak walls and higher-intensity walking. | Fit travelers can try Wulong Village - Tianbo Mansion in 3-4 hours. Expect narrow steps and steep sections. Not ideal for older visitors or knee problems. | Most ordinary travelers can skip Yangjiajie and choose Ten-Mile Gallery + Huangshizhai or rest. |
| Ten-Mile Gallery | An easy sightseeing line with symbolic peaks such as Old Man Gathering Herbs and Three Sisters Peak. | Take the mini-train one way and walk back, or take the mini-train both ways. Good for afternoons and low-energy days. | If too crowded, skip it and save time for Tianzi Mountain or Golden Whip Stream. |
| Huangshizhai | A classic high viewing platform inside the Forest Park. It is good for panoramic peak-forest photography but requires cableway or many steps. | Use half a day if you have extra time. Photographers may go early morning or late afternoon. | If you already visited Yuanjiajie and Tianzi Mountain, Huangshizhai is optional. Use the time for recovery or Tianmen Mountain. |
| Tianmen Mountain Cableway | The scenic opening of Tianmen Mountain. Views shift from city to river valley to cliff face. | Book the smoothest available Line A/B/C in advance. In peak season, reserve 1-3 days ahead. | If the cableway is closed or weather is poor, use Dayong Ancient Town, Tujia Folk Customs Park or postpone Tianmen Mountain. |
| Glass Skywalk / Guigu Plank Road | Cliff-side walking experience. The glass skywalk is the thrill point; Guigu Plank Road offers better mountain and cloud views. | Do not focus only on the glass section. Follow the east or west loop and look into the canyon. If afraid of heights, keep to the inner side or skip glass. | In rain, fog or fear of heights, reduce cliff paths and keep Tianmen Cave and cableway. |
| Tianmen Cave and 999 Steps | The natural arch is the symbolic finale of Tianmen Mountain. The 999 steps create the classic Stairway to Heaven image. | Take escalators when available if energy is limited. Walk the steps only if knees and weather are good. Allow 1-1.5 hours. | If the cave area is closed, keep the mountain loop and return by official transport. |
| Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge | A high-adrenaline glass bridge over a deep canyon, suitable for visitors wanting a special visual and psychological experience. | Book timed tickets. Visit early or late to avoid crowds. Combine with the canyon trail if weather and energy are good. | If afraid of heights or rainy, switch to Huanglong Cave or Baofeng Lake. |
| Huanglong Cave | A large cave system with underground river sections, stalactites and lighting. It is the best rain-proof attraction in Wulingyuan. | Allow 2-3 hours. Good when outdoor visibility is bad. Wear non-slip shoes because the cave can be wet. | If caves feel too enclosed, choose Baofeng Lake instead. |
| Baofeng Lake | A relaxed lake and boat trip surrounded by peaks. It is easier than a canyon or mountain day. | Use it as an afternoon or recovery choice. Good for families and low-energy travelers. | If weather is very bad, return to hotel rest. |
| Qingjiang Butterfly Cliff | A relaxed boat-based river day with cliffs, water views and a slower pace. | Use as a buffer day before Enshi Grand Canyon. It is easier than hiking and helps reset after Zhangjiajie. | If boat operation is affected, use Enshi city food/rest day or Tusi City / Daughter City. |
| Enshi Grand Canyon - Qixingzhai | The most dramatic high-view canyon section in Enshi. Focus on Yizhuxiang, cliff walkway, Welcoming Pine and peak clusters. | Start early from Enshi or stay near the visitor center. Allow 5-6 hours including transport, shuttle and rest. | If low on energy, split Qixingzhai and Yunlong Ground Fissure into two days or choose only Qixingzhai highlights. |
| Yunlong Ground Fissure | A lower, wetter canyon walk with waterfalls, narrow walls and strong gorge atmosphere. | Visit after Qixingzhai or on the next day. Wear non-slip shoes and expect wet stairs. Allow 2-3 hours. | In heavy rain, skip the fissure and rest in Enshi city. |
| Pingshan Canyon | The clearest-water day of this route, famous for narrow gorge views and floating-boat photos. | Confirm opening, water level and boat operation before leaving. Start very early from Enshi or stay in Hefeng. | If water is muddy, weather is poor or tickets are unavailable, replace with Suobuya Stone Forest, Tenglong Cave or an Enshi rest day. |
| Suobuya Stone Forest / Tenglong Cave | Good supplementary nature options. Suobuya is outdoor limestone forest; Tenglong Cave is a large cave-and-underground-river system. | Choose Suobuya for outdoor karst stone formations; choose Tenglong Cave for rain backup or if staying near Lichuan. | If tired, use Day 12 as a city recovery day instead. |

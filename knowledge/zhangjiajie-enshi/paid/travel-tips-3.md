## Risks and Backup Plans

| Risk | Possible Impact | Backup Plan |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rain / fog | Yuanjiajie and Tianzi Mountain visibility drops; glass bridge and cliff paths become less enjoyable; Pingshan water may turn muddy. | In Zhangjiajie, switch to Huanglong Cave / Baofeng Lake. On Tianmen Mountain, reduce glass skywalks. In Enshi, switch to Tenglong Cave / Suobuya. Cancel or delay Pingshan if water quality is poor. |
| Low energy | Consecutive mountain walking may cause knee pain and fatigue, affecting the second half of the route. | Reduce Yangjiajie, use escalators on Tianmen Mountain, split Enshi Grand Canyon into two days, avoid same-day round trip to Pingshan or cancel it. |
| Fear of heights | Glass skywalks, glass bridge and cliff paths may feel stressful. | Skip glass sections and keep cableways, valley walks, caves and lakes. Replace Grand Canyon with Huanglong Cave or Baofeng Lake. |
| Ticketing failure | Passport may not work online; time slots may sell out during holidays. | Use Trip.com / Ctrip international version, hotel front desk assistance or manual windows. Confirm 1-3 days ahead and keep backup attractions. |
| Transport delay | Zhangjiajie-Enshi and Enshi-Pingshan are long segments. | Use Day 7 only for transfer. Keep Day 13 flexible. Stay near the departure transport node before leaving. |
| Restaurant changes | Restaurants may close, relocate, queue heavily or change rating. | Search Chinese names again on Dianping/Ctrip. Use backup categories: sanxiaguo, Tujia cuisine, hezha, kang potatoes, lamb gege. |

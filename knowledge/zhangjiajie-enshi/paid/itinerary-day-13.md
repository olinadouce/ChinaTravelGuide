## Day 13 | Flexible Enshi Buffer Day

| Item | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Today’s Focus | Make-up sightseeing, recovery and departure preparation. Do not add high-risk long-distance attractions. |
| Route | Enshi city / nearby easy point |
| Overnight | Enshi / Yichang / Wuhan direction |
| Estimated Cost | RMB 300-1,800 / USD 42-250 |

Recommended route (follow in this order)

Morning | Sleep in, laundry, packing, photo sorting or missed city food.

Option A | Make up a missed light attraction such as Daughter City, Tusi City or a short city walk.

Option B | If Pingshan failed and conditions are now good, attempt it only if you have enough time and a reliable car.

Afternoon | Move closer to the departure station/airport if needed.

Evening | Final Enshi meal: hezha, Tujia dishes, kang potatoes or a safer chain/restaurant near hotel.

Transport and distance notes: Use this as a buffer against earlier delays. If departing from Yichang/Wuhan later, consider moving partway by train today.

Dining suggestions: Eat specific local dishes rather than random convenience food: hezha, cured hoof, shefan, kang potatoes, lamb gege or Enshi beef/noodles.

Accommodation suggestions: Stay near the station or city center depending on departure time.

International visitor notes: Do not schedule a remote canyon before departure unless the next day is fully flexible.

Backup plan: If weather is poor, keep this as a rest day. The value of Day 13 is route stability.

14-Day Zhangjiajie & Enshi Canyon Nature Route Route 4 Handbook for International Visitors

Zhangjiajie - Wulingyuan - Tianmen Mountain - Enshi Grand Canyon - Pingshan Canyon

This handbook is written directly for international visitors. It is designed as an actionable nature-route guide, not a general sightseeing article. A traveler should be able to use it to choose accommodation areas, understand how to move between cities and scenic areas, follow a daily sightseeing order, select concrete restaurants, estimate costs and handle backup plans. All prices are reference prices only. Before departure, check final prices and opening conditions again on official platforms, Trip.com, Ctrip, Qunar, Dianping, hotel front desks or scenic-area ticket counters.

| Basic Information | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Route Theme | Quartz-sandstone peak forests, cliff paths, glass walkways, mountain cableways, canyon fissures, karst caves, underground rivers, Qingjiang River water scenery and western Hubei Tujia mountain landscapes. |
| Recommended Duration | 14 days / 13 nights |
| Route Entry / Exit | Recommended entry from Zhangjiajie; exit from Enshi, Yichang or Wuhan. The route can also be operated in reverse. |
| Suitable For | First-time international nature travelers in China, photographers, light-hiking travelers, couples, families and small groups. Not ideal for visitors who are extremely afraid of heights and cannot walk for extended periods. |
| Best Seasons | April-June and September-November. Spring has clouds, mist and streams; autumn usually has clearer views. Summer is humid and crowded; winter may bring fog, ice, snow or cableway adjustments. |
| Difficulty | Moderate to moderate-high. There is no high-altitude sickness risk, but the route includes consecutive mountain days, scenic shuttle buses, cliff paths, stairs and long-distance transfers. |
| Exchange Rate Used | USD 1 approximately RMB 7.2. USD amounts are rounded and for budgeting only. |

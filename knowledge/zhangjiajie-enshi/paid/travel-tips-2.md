## Practical Tips for International Visitors

Documents and ticketing: Carry the original passport. Hotel check-in, high-speed rail, scenic-area ticket windows and real-name checks may require the physical passport. If an online page does not accept passports, use Trip.com, Ctrip international version, hotel front desk help or the manual ticket window.

Payment and cash: Use Alipay as the main payment tool and WeChat Pay as backup. Carry Visa/Mastercard and RMB 500-1,500 in cash. Mountain guesthouses, drivers, small restaurants and rural shops may prefer cash or direct transfer.

Language and navigation: Save all hotel, attraction, station and restaurant names in Chinese. Showing Chinese addresses to drivers is more reliable than English names. Prepare phrases such as: "Please take me to this hotel," "I need to buy a ticket with my passport," and "Please help me call a car."

Gear: Bring non-slip walking shoes, raincoat, waterproof phone bag, power bank, sunscreen, mosquito repellent, motion-sickness medicine, stomach medicine and a light jacket. Zhangjiajie and Enshi both involve many wet stairs and mountain paths.

## Practical Tips for International Visitors

Documents and ticketing: Carry your original passport with you. Hotel check-in, high-speed rail, scenic-area ticket windows and real-name reservation checks may require the physical passport. If an online ticketing page does not accept passports, try Trip.com, 12306, Ctrip international version, hotel front desk assistance or the scenic-area manual ticket counter.

Payment and cash: Use Alipay as the main payment tool and WeChat Pay as a backup. Carry Visa/Mastercard and RMB 800-2,000 in cash because mountain towns, small restaurants and drivers may rely more on cash or domestic payment methods.

Altitude and health: Jiuzhaigou, Huanglong and Mount Siguniang are highland areas. Walk slowly, avoid alcohol, keep warm and do not overfill the first high-altitude day. Travelers with heart disease, serious hypertension or respiratory conditions should consult a doctor before this route.

Language and navigation: Save all hotel, attraction, station and airport names in Chinese. Showing drivers Chinese names is more reliable than English names. Prepare phrases such as: “I need to buy a ticket with my passport,” “Please take me to this hotel,” and “Please help me call the driver.”

Luggage and gear: Bring non-slip shoes, raincoat, warm layers, sunscreen, sunglasses, power bank, motion-sickness medicine and basic stomach medicine. A raincoat is more practical than an umbrella in Jiuzhaigou, Huanglong and Mount Siguniang.

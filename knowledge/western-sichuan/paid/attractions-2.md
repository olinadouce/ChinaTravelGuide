## Main Scenic Highlights and Backup Plans

| Attraction / Area | Priority | What Is Most Worth Seeing | Backup / Shortened Plan |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Chengdu Panda Base | Most Worth Seeing | Active morning panda behavior, young panda areas and bamboo-lined walking paths. | If crowded or short on time, replace with People’s Park or Wangjianglou Park as a light buffer. |
| Jiuzhaigou - Five-Flower Lake | Must See | One of Jiuzhaigou’s most representative lakes, with layered blue-green water, lake-bottom textures and strong autumn reflections. | If crowded, take wide-angle photos first and move on toward Pearl Shoal. |
| Jiuzhaigou - Pearl Shoal Waterfall | Must See | Water spreads across a travertine shoal before dropping into the waterfall; excellent for dynamic water scenery. | In rain or slippery conditions, shorten the boardwalk section. |
| Jiuzhaigou - Nuorilang Waterfall | Must See | One of the park’s landmark waterfalls and a key transfer node between the three valleys. | If energy is low, keep Nuorilang and reduce Shuzheng Valley walking. |
| Jiuzhaigou - Long Lake / Five-Color Pond | Highly Recommended | Zechawa Valley’s high-lake combination. Long Lake is open and broad; Five-Color Pond is small but has concentrated color. | If time is tight, prioritize Five-Color Pond over a long stay at Long Lake. |
| Huanglong - Five-Color Pond | Must See | The core pool cluster of Huanglong and the main target of the entire Huanglong visit. High altitude requires pacing. | If altitude discomfort appears, use the cableway and see the essential Five-Color Pond area only. |
| Huanglong - Golden Sand Pavement / Zhengyan Pools | Highly Recommended | Travertine stream terraces and layered pools showing the transition of Huanglong’s landform. | See them naturally while descending. Do not climb back and forth just to complete every point. |
| Shuangqiao Valley - Redwood Forest | Must See | The endpoint of the sightseeing-bus route, with high-elevation forest and snow-mountain views. | If weather is poor, stay briefly and move down to lower viewpoints. |
| Shuangqiao Valley - Sigu Nacuo / Longzhucuo | Must See | Lake reflections, dead trees, water color and snow mountains form the most accessible classic Shuangqiao views. | If tired, keep Sigu Nacuo and skip longer side walks. |
| Changping Valley | Highly Recommended | Forests, streams, boardwalks and valley views make it the best light-hiking day in Mount Siguniang. | Walk only to Kushu Tan / lower valley sections if energy is limited. |
| Haizi Valley | Optional Advanced | Open snow-mountain viewpoints and higher hiking intensity; better for photographers and stronger walkers. | Replace with Maobiliang viewpoint or a full rest day for ordinary visitors. |
| Dujiangyan / Qingcheng Mountain | Optional Buffer | Good final-buffer options near Chengdu, combining natural scenery with easy access. | If tired or rainy, stay in Chengdu for rest, laundry, packing and a final meal. |

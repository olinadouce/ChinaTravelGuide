## Risks and Backup Plans

| Risk | Possible Impact | Backup Plan |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Altitude discomfort | Headache, shortness of breath or fatigue may occur in Huanglong, Mount Siguniang Town, Shuangqiao, Changping and Haizi valleys. | Reduce walking, use cableway/sightseeing buses, cancel long Haizi Valley route, descend or seek medical help if needed. |
| Rain/snow/road conditions | Mountain roads such as Chengdu-Mount Siguniang and Jiuzhaigou-Huanglong may be affected. | Do not schedule attractions on transfer days. Return to Chengdu during daylight. Avoid night mountain roads in winter. |
| Crowds | Jiuzhaigou, Panda Base and Huanglong can be crowded during peak season. | Enter early, keep the core sites, and reduce non-core boardwalk sections. |
| Public transport ticketing failure | Foreign visitors may face online registration, passport recognition or sold-out ticket issues. | Use 12306/Trip.com, carry passport to staffed ticket counters, and confirm key transfers 1-3 days ahead. |
| Restaurant changes | Small-town restaurants may change hours, ratings or availability. | Search Chinese names on Dianping/Ctrip before departure and keep hotel restaurants/simple meals as backup. |
| Low energy | Back-to-back park days and highland walking can accumulate fatigue. | Use Day 5 as a half-day park visit; replace Haizi Valley with Maobiliang/rest; keep Day 13 as an insurance day. |

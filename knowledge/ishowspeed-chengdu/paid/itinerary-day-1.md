## Day 1 · Pandas, Tea Houses

| Time | Attraction / Experience | Suggested Plan | Practical Note |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 09:00–11:30 | Kuanzhai Alley | Walk the three historic lanes; try san da pao and optional panda-themed face paint. | Face paint is inspired by the stream; use reputable vendors and skin-safe products. |
| 12:00–13:30 | Sichuan snack lunch | Choose dan dan noodles, Zhong dumplings or sweet-water noodles. | Order mild spice if needed. |
| 14:00–15:30 | People’s Park & tea house | Gaiwan tea, local rap music and relaxed park observation. | Experience local Sichuan lifestyle. |
| Optional | Licensed TCM / ear-cleaning experience | Use a licensed clinic for acupuncture or Chinese Massage; choose a hygienic, reputable provider for ear cleaning. | Speed’s stream included acupuncture and ear cleaning, but these are optional personal-care activities. |
| 17:00–18:30 | Sichuan Opera performance | Drink traditional gaiwan tea and watch tea art, Sichuan Opera face-changing and fire-breathing performances. | Speed chose Heming Teahouse to watch the face-changing and fire-spitting performances. Advance reservations for this show are required to avoid sold-out seats when buying tickets on site. |
| 19:00–21:00 | Sichuan hotpot | Choose a divided pot with tomato or mushroom broth plus mild spicy broth. | Budget around CNY 100–400 per person. |

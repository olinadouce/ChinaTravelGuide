Route Highlights

Shanghai in four essential experiences

Traditional gardens, a high-energy city walk, the Bund at sunset and Lujiazui's futuristic skyline - all arranged in a route that is easy to follow.

Yu Garden & Old City

Traditional

Explore Yuyuan Bazaar, classical garden scenery, the Nine-Turn Bridge and the atmosphere of Shanghai's historic old city.

Nanjing East Road

City Walk

Walk from People's Square through the pedestrian street toward the Bund, with shopping, street photography and an optional Speed-inspired snack stop.

Bund Sunset & Night View

Skyline

Arrive before sunset to experience daylight, blue hour, illuminated historic architecture and the Pudong skyline across the river.

Lujiazui Modern City

Futuristic

See the Oriental Pearl Tower, elevated walkways, Shanghai Tower and the dense cluster of modern Pudong landmarks.

Local Flavor & Speed-Inspired Fun

Local food stops that fit naturally into the route

Try Shanghai breakfast and snack classics such as shengjianbao or xiaolongbao, then finish Day 1 with a Speed-inspired hotpot dinner. For an optional themed detour, stop for fried chicken or mildly spicy noodles near Nanjing East Road.

Travel Tips

Prepare before you start

Keep these points in mind before following the route.

💳

Payment

Set up Alipay or WeChat Pay before arrival and carry a small amount of RMB as backup.

📱

Internet

Prepare an eSIM or international roaming for maps, translation, ride-hailing and mobile payment.

👥

Crowds

The Bund, Nanjing East Road and Lujiazui are busiest in the evening. Add buffer time for walking and photos.

🌃

Night Views

Visit the Bund from late afternoon to evening to see daylight, sunset, blue hour and illumination.

🎟️

Tickets

Use official attraction channels, WeChat mini programs, Trip.com or on-site service desks. Reconfirm opening hours.

🏨

Hotel Check-in

Book with the exact passport name and carry the original passport.

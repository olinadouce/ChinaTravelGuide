4. Attraction & Booking Notes

Small preparations that prevent major friction

Ticket prices, opening hours, reservation rules and showroom availability can change. Reconfirm all operational details shortly before travel.

Yuyuan Bazaar

Free admission - 1-.5 hours

Special crowd controls may apply during lantern fairs or festivals.

Yu Garden

Paid ticket - about 1 hour

Daily ticket reference: RMB 40. Night ticket reference: RMB 188-18. Carry your passport and reconfirm opening hours.

Nanjing East Road

Free admission - 1.5- hours

It becomes very busy at night, so keep valuables secure and allow extra time for photos.

The Bund

Free admission - 1-.5 hours

Evening hours and public holidays are the most crowded, especially around core skyline viewpoints.

Huangpu River cruise

Paid ticket - about 1 hour

Fares depend on the cruise company. Confirm the pier, schedule and operator before travel.

Observation options

Paid or free - 1.5- hours

Oriental Pearl and other observation decks require tickets. Cloud, haze and rain can reduce visibility.

6. Shanghai Food Guide

Approachable Shanghainese food for first-time visitors

Benbang cuisine is known for sweet-savory seasoning, soy-based braising and polished home-style cooking. These choices are approachable for first-time visitors.

Classic Benbang

Red-braised pork belly / Hong Shao Rou

Budget: RMB 60-20 per dish
Glossy, tender and sweet-savory; a signature local dish.

Classic Benbang

Sweet-and-sour pork ribs / Tang Cu Xiao Pai

Budget: RMB 50-00 per dish
An approachable starter with balanced sweet and sour flavors.

Classic Benbang

Shanghai-style smoked fish / Xun Yu

Budget: RMB 50-20 per dish
A sweet-savory cold appetizer that is usually served before the main meal.

Seasonal soup

Yanduxian

Budget: RMB 80-60 per pot
Salted pork, fresh pork and bamboo shoots; especially popular in spring.

Noodles

Scallion oil noodles / Cong You Ban Mian

Budget: RMB 15-5 per bowl
Simple, local and ideal for a quick lunch.

Breakfast / snack

Shengjianbao

Budget: RMB 10-0 per serving
Crisp bottom, soft wrapper and juicy pork filling.

Dim sum

Xiaolongbao

Budget: RMB 25-0 per basket
Eat carefully: the soup inside can be very hot.

Speed-inspired dinner

Hotpot

Budget: RMB 120-00 per person
A fun themed finish for Day 1, especially if you want a lively, shareable meal.

8. Practical Information

Practical notes to smooth the trip

These are the most useful reminders for payment, navigation, tickets, hotel check-in and final rail departure.

Payment

Set up Alipay or WeChat Pay, link an international bank card and carry a small cash backup.

Internet

Prepare an eSIM or international roaming for maps, translation and ride-hailing.

Navigation

Save Chinese place names and addresses; they are easier for drivers and local staff to recognize.

Hotel check-in

Carry the original passport and confirm that the hotel accepts foreign guests.

Tickets

Use official channels, attraction mini programs, Trip.com or on-site service desks when needed.

Crowds & safety

Keep valuables secure and add time buffers around The Bund, Nanjing East Road and holiday periods.

High-speed rail

Use the passport name exactly and arrive 60-0 minutes before departure.

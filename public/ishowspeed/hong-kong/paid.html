4. Attraction & Booking Notes

Confirm the operating details before travel

Ticket prices, opening hours, reservation rules and attraction availability can change. Reconfirm all details shortly before departure.

Villain Hitting under the Canal Road Flyover

No admission ticket; practitioner fees vary - 30-0 min

This is a public street-based folk practice with no fixed official opening hours or standard ticket price. Practitioners may not always be present. Confirm the price before participating and ask for permission before filming.

Ocean Park Hong Kong

Adult: HK$538; Child aged 3-1: HK$269 - 4- h

Current park calendars commonly list 10:00-8:30 on operating days. Opening days, animal viewing and attraction schedules vary. Purchase tickets through the official website and check the calendar for the selected date.

Victoria Peak, Peak Tram & Sky Terrace 428

Peak public area: Free; Peak Tram adult: HK$116 return / HK$82 single; Sky Terrace 428: HK$80; Tram + Sky Terrace adult combo: HK$182 return / HK$146 single - 1.5- h

Peak Tram: 07:30-3:00 daily. Sky Terrace 428: 10:00-2:00 Monday-Friday; 08:00-2:00 Saturdays, Sundays and public holidays. Advance booking is recommended for sunset and evening visits.

Man Mo Temple

Free admission - 45-0 min

Open 08:00-8:00 daily. Opens 07:00-8:00 on the 1st and 15th days of each lunar month and on selected Chinese festivals. Religious ceremonies may affect visitor access.

Hong Kong Tramways

Adult flat fare: HK$3.30 per ride - 45-0 min

Regular tram services operate from approximately 05:00 until around midnight, but first and last departures vary by route and day. Pay when leaving through the front exit using Octopus, cash, supported bank cards or mobile payment.

Kam Shan Country Park

Free - 2- h

This is an open country park without a ticket gate or published daily opening and closing time. Visit during daylight, ideally around 07:00-8:00. Do not feed, touch, chase or approach macaques, and keep all food out of sight.

Temple Street Night Market

Free public market - 1- h

The street is publicly accessible throughout the day, but the night market generally becomes active after 19:00. The recommended visiting period is approximately 18:00-2:00. Stall numbers and opening times vary according to weather and individual vendors.

Avenue of Stars

Free - 45-0 min

Open-air public promenade with no admission gate or fixed visitor session. Temporary closures or crowd controls may apply during major events. A Symphony of Lights-is normally staged at 20:00 each evening, subject to weather warnings and operational changes.

Victoria Harbour Cruise -Star Ferry Water Tour

Dazzling Night Tour: HK$280 adult / HK$200 concessionary fare - 45 min

The night tour is scheduled for 19:45-0:30 under the timetable effective from 1 February 2026. The Tsim Sha Tsui ticket counter opens 11:30-9:45 and stops selling tickets 10 minutes before departure. Online advance purchase is recommended.

6. Hong Kong Food Guide

Local food choices for first-time visitors

Budget references are approximate and vary by restaurant, season, serving size and number of diners.

Dim Sum / Yum Cha

Har gow, siu mai, char siu bao, rice rolls and turnip cake

HK$100-400 pp
Order 2- baskets per person and share. Tea and service charges may be added separately.

Wonton Noodles

Shrimp wonton noodles, beef brisket noodles or mixed noodles

HK$55-00 per bowl
A classic quick meal. Traditional portions may be small, so larger appetites can add vegetables or extra toppings.

Hong Kong Roast Meats

Roast goose, char siu, crispy pork belly and soy-sauce chicken

HK$75-00 pp for rice; HK$220-000 for a shared goose portion
Solo travellers can order roast meat over rice, while groups can share a mixed platter or quarter goose.

Cha Chaan Teng

Macaroni soup, satay beef noodles, scrambled eggs, toast, sandwiches and milk tea

HK$35-00 pp
Breakfast and afternoon-tea sets are usually the best value. Iced drinks may cost slightly more.

Bakery Snacks

Egg tart, pineapple bun, pineapple bun with butter and cocktail bun

HK$25-100 per item
Ideal for a quick snack. Egg tarts are best eaten warm, while pineapple buns normally contain no pineapple.

Cart Noodles

Noodles with fish balls, beef brisket, radish, mushrooms, chicken wings or vegetables

HK$55-00 per bowl
Choose the noodle type, soup base and toppings. Check ingredients carefully if you do not eat offal or pork products.

Street Snacks and Desserts

Curry fish balls, fish siu mai, egg waffles, tofu pudding and mango pomelo sago

HK$20-0 per item
Good for sharing while exploring markets. Smaller stalls may prefer cash or local electronic payment methods.

Claypot Rice

Chinese sausage and pork-rib rice, chicken and mushroom rice or preserved-meat rice

HK$85-80 per pot
Cooked to order and especially popular in cooler weather. Mix in the soy sauce and try the crispy rice crust at the bottom.

Dai Pai Dong / Seafood

Cantonese stir-fries, salt-and-pepper squid, steamed fish, prawns or lobster noodles

HK$120-50 pp for stir-fries; HK$250-000 pp for seafood
Best for groups. For live seafood, confirm the weight, unit price, cooking charge and estimated total before ordering.

8. Practical Information

Useful reminders before departure

Payment

Use Octopus, widely accepted cards and supported mobile payments; carry small cash for some markets.

Internet

Use an eSIM or roaming.

Navigation

Use MTR / bus apps and save English plus Chinese place names.

Hotel check-in

Carry the original passport.

Wildlife

Never feed macaques or expose food at Kam Shan.

Crowds

Avoid following or surrounding public figures and keep station access clear.

Cross-border travel

Check immigration documents and allow extra time.
